WHAT IS MERENGUE?

Merengue is a dance music genre originated in the Dominican Republic at the end of the 19th century. It is very popular throughout the Americas, where it is considered, along with salsa, as one of the great dance genres that distinguish the Latin American genre. It is also very popular in part of Europe, like Spain, among other latitudes. The merengue was inscribed on November 30, 2016 in the representative list of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity of Unesco. In its origins, the Dominican merengue was interpreted with string instruments (bandurria and / or guitar). Years later, the string instruments were replaced by the accordion, thus conforming, together with the güira and the tambora, the instrumental structure of the typical merengue ensemble. This set, with its three instruments, represents the synthesis of the three cultures that shaped the idiosyncrasy of Dominican culture. The European influence comes to be represented by the accordion, the African by the drum, which is a drum with two patches, and the Taíno or aboriginal by the güira.

Although in some areas of the Dominican Republic, especially in the Cibao and in the Northwest sub-region, there are still typical sets with similar characteristics to those pioneers, this rhythm was evolving throughout the 20th century. First, with the introduction of new instruments such as the saxophone and later with the appearance of orchestras with complex instrumental wind sections. The evolution of decent letter meringue to liven up one of its paths. From then on, it spread very quickly throughout the country. In 1875, President Ulises Francisco Espaillat (known for his writings against merengue) began a campaign against merengue for his dances and explicit lyrics, but it was totally useless, since the dance had already taken over the Cibao, where he became strong point that is associated today with this region as the cradle of merengue.
