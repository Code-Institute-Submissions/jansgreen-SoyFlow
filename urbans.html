WHAT IS URBAN DOMINICAN MUSIC?

Urban music (or contemporary urban) is a term used in the Dominican Republic to refer in general to contemporary popular music. Genres such as Dominican rap (or hip hop), El Dembow and Trap are included under this term. Occasionally, urban music is used more specifically to refer in general.

It began to be called urban music (or urban music) in the 1980s to music such as reggaeton, rap, dembow that used a more digital production than organic and that was closer to a general taste; starting in Puerto Rico and shortly after in the Dominicans they conditioned it to their culture. There is, in the Dominican Republic, a whole series of radio stations specialized in this type of music. This radio format is known as Urban Radio. Some artists associated with this musical trend are Zurdo Cartiel, Los Pepe, El Alfa, Chimbala, Musicologist The Book, Monkey Black, The Materialist, The Classical Major, Mozart La Para, Milka The Hardest, The Unsurpassed, Bulova, Liro Shaq, La Manta, Yomel el Meloso, Clon876, among others.​
