WHAT IS BACHATA?

In the early 1920s in Cuba, the term "bachata" was used to define a type of festive social gathering. Etymologically, the word bachata is of African origin and designates the spree, the revelry and the party, according to Fernando Ortiz Fernández.

The bachata was synonymous with popular recreation, patio or street party, and this links it with its African background. On the other hand, its connection with Spain is noted in the fandango, a cultural manifestation to which in the words of Marcio Veloz Maggiolo "Almost all chroniclers who play this theme refer to an open holiday and not to music". [Citation needed ] In Santo Domingo, the oldest reference about bachata as a name to designate parties of any kind of music - and not as a musical genre - is found in documents dating from 1922 and 1927. The 19224 report refers to the man common of the town of Sabaneta, in the Northwest Line and describes in this way what we can find there.
