WHAT IS BACHATA?

In the early 1920s in Cuba, the term "bachata" was used to define a type of festive social gathering. Etymologically, the word bachata is of African origin and designates the spree, the revelry and the party, according to Fernando Ortiz Fernández.

The bachata was synonymous with popular recreation, patio or street party, and this links it with its African background. On the other hand, its connection with Spain is noted in the fandango, a cultural manifestation to which in the words of Marcio Veloz Maggiolo "Almost all chroniclers who play this theme refer to an open holiday and not to music". [Citation needed ] In Santo Domingo, the oldest reference about bachata as a name to designate parties of any kind of music - and not as a musical genre - is found in documents dating from 1922 and 1927. WHERE IT STARTED?

Bachata is a dance music genre originally from the Dominican Republic, in what is called urban folklore. It is considered as a derivative of the rhythmic bolero, with influences from other styles such as the Cuban son and the merengue.

HOW DANCED IT?

The basic dance movement is a series of simple steps that produce a movement from front to back, or from side to side. A possible scheme would be the following: Counting that the basic step is 4 times.

With the right foot a chassé is made to the right, the 1 (starting with feet together we simply open with the right foot to the right side). The 2 left foot joins the right one again. On 3 we repeat the first step (open right foot). And on the 4th we get back together (the woman joins her left foot on tip) giving a hip blow up to the side where we come from (in this case we would give it to the left), the woman will make it more pronounced. And the same sequence is repeated to the other side, but as we now go to the left side, we open with the left foot. We can imagine that we move all the time in the same line but not forward and backward, but from side to side. The basic step is simple, the difficulty is in maintaining the passage of legs, while moving the arms (this movement of arms has a wide repertoire because the man will indicate to the woman turns, passes of arms in front, behind, curls , throwing arms) at the same time you dance with one person, it is a dance of two. The woman must learn to make certain figures with her arms. In bachata the figure of the hands is the same as in sauce, because in this sense they come very hand in hand. The man directs the woman with movements of arms and hands and is not an easy task, because he must know how to guide her. A man who does not lead well will not be good in bachata, just as it happens in salsa or tango. The woman must know how to follow the man and interpret what he orders with each arm movement. Bachata is a sensual and seductive dance like tango, the kizomba.
